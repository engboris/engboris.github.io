Current works as a PhD student

I work on two projects :

  • With Thomas Seiller : The “Transcendental Syntax” project of Jean-Yves Girard aiming at a reconstruction of logical entities (LL proof-nets, formulas, correctness criterion, truth/provability) through computational processes. A computational model called “Stars and constellations” (which is basically the computational content of first-order clausal resolution: stars are clauses, constellations are programs) is used as a system of resolution of addresses where proof-nets are seen as a constellation describing the spatial position of its parts. A type is described as a set of constellations passing a certain number of tests (actually dual constellations with which they interact). We thus obtain a more refined concept of type with possible applications in the study of computational complexity.

  • With Damiano Mazza : How can the Geometry of Interaction provide results about the space complexity of lambda-terms. We also have an approximation of lambda-terms by (affine) polyadic approximations (used by Mazza which are basically deterministic resource terms i.e we use lists instead of multisets). This is similar to how boolean circuits approximate Turing machines (Cook-Levin theorem). Moreover, polyadic approximations have non-idempotent intersection types as simple types where the size and the depth of their derivation tree are related to the time and space used in the reduction similarly to boolean circuits (Borodin’s works). We would like to relate the computational complexity of lambda terms to the ones of Turing machines in order to provide characterizations of classes within a lambda-calculi.

Other interests : ludics, non-classical approaches in Computational Complexity, linguistics (syntax, semantics, pragmatics), quantum computation, interdisciplinary connections, social-psychological-biological understanding of logic, epistemology, foundations of Logic and Computation
