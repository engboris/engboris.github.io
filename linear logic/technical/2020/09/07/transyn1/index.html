Transcendental Syntax for the linear logician through examples

September 7, 2020
linear logic technical

Prerequisite: linear logic, proof-nets, correctness criterion, first-order term unification

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the Transcendental Syntax about? The Transcendental Syntax is a programme initiated by Girard which can be seen as the successor of his Geometry of Interaction (GoI) programme. In the same idea, it aims at giving a fully computational foundation for logic where entities such as formulas, proofs, correctness, truth are reconstructed from scratch with computational objects. It can be said to be free from prejudices because we don’t have preconceptions about how the logical objects should be but we still want some fundamental properties in order to have a coherent logic.
  • Where does the model of stars and constellation come from? The GoI began with a mathematical study of the cut-elimination procedure in the setting of operator algebras. In the third article of GoI, Girard discovered a simplification with the model of flows based on first-order unification. The stellar resolution is simply an extension of this model.
  • Is it related to any other works? The stellar resolution works with stars which can be connected to each other like atoms or molecules and their interaction cause an annhilation producing a reaction. It seems that this model is able to simulate abstract tile assembly models which are used in DNA computing but also the combinatorial model of Wang tiles. It can also be seen as a generalization of the model of flows used in GoI and of Seiller’s interaction graphs.
  • Why is it interesting? In the Transcendental Syntax, logic is studied with finite objects without reference to the infinity. Moreover, it generalises flows and interaction graphs which have interesting applications in implicit computational complexity. The Transcendental Syntax has a very general notion of typing by confronting a computational object to a set of tests (usually finite) in order to certify/label it. The more interesting point is that the third article of Transcendental Syntax describes how to construct a model for first-order logic thus giving a computational content to first-order logic in the sense of the Curry-Howard correspondence.

Stars and constellations

We define rays by the grammar $r ::= +a(t) \mid -a(t) \mid t$ where $+/-$ are polarties, $a$ is a function symbol from a set of colours and $t$ is a first-order term.

A star is a multiset of rays $\sigma = [r_1, …, r_n]$ and a constellation $\Sigma = \sigma_1 + … + \sigma_m$ is a multiset of stars. We consider stars to be equivalent up to renaming.

This is very similar to clauses and logic programs. We can even encode them in our model. For instance, we have the following constellations: $\Sigma_\mathbb{N}^{n,m} = [-add(0, y, y)]+[-add(s(x), y, s(z)), +add(x, y, z)]+[+add(s^n(0), s^m(0), r), r]$ $\Sigma_{family} = [+parent(d, j)]+[+parent(z, d)]+[+parent(s, z)]+[+parent(n, s)]+$ $[+ancestor(x, y), -parent(x, y)]+[+ancestor(x, z), -parent(x, y), -ancestor(y, z)]+$ $[-ancestor(j, r), r]$

Execution

The normalization or execution $\mathtt{exec}(\Sigma)$ of a constellation $\Sigma$ construct the set of correct (the underlying unification problem doesn’t fail) saturated (no stars can be added to extend it) diagrams and actualise them all in order to produce a new constellation called the normal form. For $\Sigma_\mathbb{N}^{2,2}$, one can check that beside $\delta$, all other diagrams fail so $\mathtt{exec}(\Sigma_\mathbb{N}^{2,2}) = [s(s(s(s(0))))]$. If the set of such correct saturated diagrams is finite, the constellation is said to be strongly normalising.

Interpretation of the computional content of MLL

Intuition. The idea comes from the GoI: the $⅋/\otimes$ cuts can be seen as administrative/inessential cuts since all they do is basically a rewiring on the premises of the $⅋$ and $\otimes$ nodes connected together. By eliminating all such cuts, we end up with a set of axioms connected by cuts. These axioms represent the essential and computational part of a proof-structure. We only have to encode axioms and cuts.

We translate $\mathcal{S}$ into stars in the following way:

  1. We associate a first-order term representing an addresses to all conclusions of axioms, relatively to the conclusions of the proof-structure (without considering cuts). We get $p_{A_1^\bot ⅋ A_1}(\mathtt{l} \cdot x)(x),\quad p_{A_1^\bot ⅋ A_1}(\mathtt{r} \cdot x)(x),\quad p_{A_2 \otimes A_3^\bot}(\mathtt{l} \cdot x)(x),\quad p_{A_2 \otimes A_3^\bot}(\mathtt{r} \cdot x)(x),\quad p_{A_2^\bot}(x),\quad p_{A_3}(x)$.

  2. Two addresses linked by an axiom link form a binary star. For instance, we have the axiom star: $[p_{A_1^\bot ⅋ A_1}(\mathtt{l} \cdot x)(x), p_{A_1^\bot ⅋ A_1}(\mathtt{r} \cdot x)(x)]$.

  3. Two formulas A and B connected by a cut link form a binary star $[p_A(x), p_B(x)]$.

  4. All axiom stars are coloured in the colour “+c” (positive computational) and the cut stars in the colour “-c” (negative computational) in order to allows interaction with the cuts. The cuts will identify two addresses.

The proof-structure becomes the constellation: $ [\color{Red}{+c.p_{A_1^\bot ⅋ A_1}(\mathtt{l} \cdot x)(x)},\color{Red}{+c.p_{A_1^\bot ⅋ A_1}(\mathtt{r} \cdot x)(x)}]+ [\color{Red}{+c.p_{A_2 \otimes A_3^\bot}(\mathtt{l} \cdot x)(x)}, \color{Red}{+c.p_{A_2^\bot}(x)}]+ [\color{Red}{+c.p_{A_2 \otimes A_3^\bot}(\mathtt{r} \cdot x)(x)}, \color{Red}{+c.p_{A_3}(x)}]+$ $[\color{Brown}{-c.p_{A_1^\bot ⅋ A_1}(x)}, \color{Brown}{-c.p_{A_2 \otimes A_3^\bot}(x)}]$.

Interpretation of the logical content of MLL

The correctness criterion of Danos-Regnier

The upper part made of axioms appearing in a proof-structure is called the vehicle. The set of Danos-Regnier switching graphs can be divided into two parts: the upper part (vehicle) is the tested and the lower part (basically a set of syntax trees) is a test against the vehicle. The vehicle holds the computational part of a proof and the test its logical part (type/formula).

If by connecting a vehicle to a set of tests, we satisfy specific criterion then the vehicle is certified (as correct in the case of Danos-Regnier). Interpretation of formulas

By the Curry-Howard correspondence, formulas have a role similar to types in programming. If our constellations are seen as computational objects which can be executed, then, formulas are classes of constellations characterising a computational behaviour. We call these classes conducts in our framework.

  • Atoms. We define atoms with a basis of interpretation $\Phi$ associating of each type variable $X_i$ a distinct conduct $\Phi(X_i)$.
  • Negation. First, we need to choose a criterion of orthogonality opposing a class and its opposite (representing an opposite behaviour). We choose the strong normalisation. If we have a set of constellation $\mathbf{A}$, its dual type, written $\mathbf{A}^\bot$, is the set of all constellations which are strongly normalising when interaction with the constellations of $\mathbf{A}$.
  • Tensor The tensor $\mathbf{A} \otimes \mathbf{B}$ of two formulas is constructed by pairing all the constellations of $\mathbf{A}$ with the ones of $\mathbf{B}$ by using a disjoint union of constellations.
  • Par and implication The par and linear implication are defined from the tensor and the orthogonal : $A ⅋ B = (A^\bot \otimes B^\bot)^\bot$ and $A \multimap B = A^\bot ⅋ B$.
